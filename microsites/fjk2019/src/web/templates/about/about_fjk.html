Saluton!

For the first time in the history of the movement of speakers, learners, and advocates of the international language Esperanto in the country, the 1st Philippine Esperanto Youth Congress will be held in University of the Philippines Diliman, Quezon City, on April 23-26, 2020.

This historic congress shall mark the culmination of seven years of effort of Esperanto advocates in the country and the forward step towards further growth and integration of the national Esperanto community, and shall provide speakers, learners, and advocates coming from different parts of the country the opportunity to interact, learn, and work together in a series of social and educational activities.

As Filipino Esperantists have never convened on a national scale since the movement was established in 2013, this Congress will be the first appropriate opportunity to consolidate the Esperanto movement in the Philippines and bring them together from all regions of the country. This will be avenues for people coming from diverse fields of interests and expertise, to freely socialize and exchange information and opinions on a wide range of topics. Most importantly, lasting initiatives for the local Esperanto movement may also be born here.

The Congress is a highly anticipated event in the international Esperanto community. Hailed as one of the "rising stars" of the Asian Esperanto movement, the Esperanto community in the country is closely observed by key advocates across the world. Therefore, as a concrete manifestation of the movementâ€™s progress, the Congress is expected to draw the attendance of speakers and advocates of the language from other countries, most of which also aim to visit the country for tourism and get to know the members of the local community for the first time. The presence of international guests would provide a truly international experience for Filipinos and open unprecedented ways for them to create new networks, without having to leave the country. Most importantly, this would amplify the magnitude and importance of the event, as this would also create revenues and tourism in the vicinity of the venue.

Lastly, the Congress shall become an impetus towards a wider public and institutional knowledge and recognition of Esperanto as a living language for communication in everyday life, eventually drawing the interest of many people from different walks of life to learn it and benefit from its many advantages.
