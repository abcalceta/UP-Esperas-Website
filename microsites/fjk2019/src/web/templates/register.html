Registration Guidelines

Welcome to the First Philippine Esperanto Youth Congress! We would like to thank you for your interest in this event. Before proceeding with the registration, please read the following guidelines.

Who can join?

Participation is open to any person of any age, Philippine or foreign national, who has at least a basic command of Esperanto and is interested to learn more of the language and its community and culture, participate in the activities of the congress, and actively interact with other participants.

Are there any fees?

Yes. The registration fee for the congress depends on three main factors: the individual's age at the time of registration, the individual's country of origin, and the period in which registration and payment are made. Individuals who register early can avail of discounted fees. There is also a separate category for individuals who wish to financially support the Congress, and separate fees for foreign participants.

The fee includes the participant's kit and access to all programs (unless otherwise indicated in the congress booklet). It does not include lodging, other meals, the excursion, and insurances.

The registration fee is strictly non-transferable and non-refundable, regardless of whether the participant fails to attend the Congress through their own volition or due to any other cause, or if the Congress is cancelled or postponed for any reason.

Registration Details

There are different categories for participants

Undergraduate Students and Below are individuals in undergraduate (college) level, high school (junior and senior), vocational schools, and below at the time of registration. It is subdivided into two subcategories, A1 (for senior high school students and below) and A2 (for undergraduate students and equivalent). Registrants in this category are required to send a photo/scanned copy of their school ID and latest certificate of registration (if applicable) to avail of the discount.

Workers, Graduate Students, or Equivalent are individuals working or practicing a profession at the time of registration. Also includes students in graduate level (master’s degree/Ph.D.) and unemployed individuals. It is further subdivided to B1 (for individuals aged 35 and below), and B2 (for individuals aged above 35).

Moral Participants are individuals who will not participate in the congress itself, but want to support the congress financially. The fee is half (50%) of the regular period registration fee of category B.1 (for both Filipino and foreign participants). They shall be given due acknowledgment in the congress booklet.

Congress Patrons are individuals who want to support the congress financially, besides joining the congress, by paying a larger rate than the normal rates. The fee is 2.5 times the regular registration fee of category B.1 (for Filipino and foreign participants). They may opt to participate in the congress itself or not, and they shall be given due acknowledgment in the congress booklet, and will receive a special gift.

Note that participants aged 12 years old and below and accompanied by their parents/guardians can join the congress free of charge, however they will not be provided their own Congress Kit (except for name tags) and must pay additional fees if they will avail of lodging, the excursion, and meals.
