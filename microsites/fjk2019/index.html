The First Philippine Esperanto Youth Congress

April 23-26, 2020
Manila, Philippines
expand_more

About

The Philippine Esperanto Youth (FEJ), in cooperation with the Esperanto Society of the University of the Philippines (UP Esperanto-Societo/UP Esperas) will be organizing the first ever Philippine Esperanto Youth Conference (PEYC), which will take place in 2020 in Manila, Philippines.

This historic congress shall mark the culmination of the seven years of effort of Esperanto advocates in the country and the forward step towards the further growth and integration of the national Esperanto community, and shall provide speakers, learners, and advocates coming from different parts of the country the opportunity to interact, learn, and work together in a series of social and educational activities.

With the theme The Esperanto Movement: fresh start in a multicultural Philippines, the congress will serve a unique and grand role in convening the Esperanto youth for the first time across the country. Aside from sessions on the Philippine Esperanto movement, there will also be cultural talks, an Esperanto crash course, fun social activities, and an excursion to some historical or natural parts around Manila.

Why Hold a Congress?

The Congress, a major milestone for the local Esperanto movement, is actually highly anticipated in the international Esperanto community. Already long overdue, it is expected to draw the attendance of speakers and advocates of the language from other countries, most of which aim to tour the country and get to know with members of the local Esperanto community, providing a truly international experience for Filipinos without leaving the country.

This Congress will also be the most appropriate opportunity to consolidate the Esperanto movement in the Philippines and bring together local speakers and advocates from all regions of the country. This will be avenues for everyone, coming from diverse fields of interests and expertise, to freely socialize and exchange information and opinions on a wide range of topics. Most important, lasting initiatives for the local Esperanto movement may also be born here.

Furthermore, if successfully organized, the Congress shall be an impetus towards a wider public and institutional knowledge and recognition of Esperanto as a living language for egalitarian interethnic communication, eventually drawing the interest of many people from different walks of life to learn the language and be part of the national Esperanto community.

Local Organizing Committee

Albert Garrido
Organizing Committee President
Director for Volunteer Staff

Albert started learning Esperanto in March 2013 and soon afterwards started to get involved in the Esperanto movement in the local and international level. He is currently the Coordinator of Asia of the World Esperanto Youth Organization (TEJO), and has attended a few international Esperanto conventions in some countries in Asia. He also has a bachelor’s degree in Geography and wrote his undergraduate thesis examining the state of the Asian Esperanto movement.

Carl Dizon
Director for Registration

Carl is the incumbent president of UP Esperanto-Societo, the first and only university-based Esperanto organization in the country. He is currently the head researcher of an e-trike study and is taking his Masters in Electrical Engineering at the University of the Philippines.

Jubert Cabrezos
Director for Information and Publicity

Jubert is a poet, artist, and Esperantist from Medina, Misamis Oriental. He earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in Communication Arts at the Philippine Women’s University. He worked in the National Secretariat of the College Editors Guild of the Philippines for over 3 years. He currently lives in North Caloocan with his family.

Elizabeth Lavadia
Director for External Relations

Elizabeth is an instructor for the Italian language at the University of the Philippines, Diliman. She took a C2 level short course on the Italian Language and Culture at Università per Stranieri di Siena from April to June 2019. She graduated Magna cum Laude in 2018 and has a Bachelor of Arts degree in European Languages from the University of the Philippines, Diliman.
