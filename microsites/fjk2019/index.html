The First Philippine Esperanto Youth Congress

April 23-26, 2020
Manila, Philippines
expand_more

About

The Philippine Esperanto Youth (FEJ), in cooperation with the Esperanto Society of the University of the Philippines (UP Esperanto-Societo/UP Esperas) will be organizing the first ever Philippine Esperanto Youth Conference (FJK), which will take place in 2020 in Manila, Philippines.

This historic congress shall mark the culmination of the seven years of effort of Esperanto advocates in the country and the forward step towards the further growth and integration of the national Esperanto community, and shall provide speakers, learners, and advocates coming from different parts of the country the opportunity to interact, learn, and work together in a series of social and educational activities.

Local Organizing Committee

Albert Garrido
Organizing Committee President
Director for Volunteer Staff

Albert started learning Esperanto in March 2013 and soon afterwards started to get involved in the Esperanto movement in the local and international level. He is currently the Coordinator of Asia of the World Esperanto Youth Organization (TEJO), and has attended a few international Esperanto conventions in some countries in Asia. He also has a bachelor’s degree in Geography and wrote his undergraduate thesis examining the state of the Asian Esperanto movement.

Carl Dizon
Director for Registration

Carl is the incumbent president of UP Esperanto-Societo, the first and only university-based Esperanto organization in the country. He is currently the head researcher of an e-trike study and is taking his Masters in Electrical Engineering at the University of the Philippines.

Jubert Cabrezos
Director for Information and Publicity

Jubert is a poet, artist, and Esperantist from Medina, Misamis Oriental. He earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in Communication Arts at the Philippine Women’s University. He worked in the National Secretariat of the College Editors Guild of the Philippines for over 3 years. He currently lives in North Caloocan with his family.

Elizabeth Lavadia
Director for External Relations

Elizabeth is an instructor for the Italian language at the University of the Philippines, Diliman. She took a C2 level short course on the Italian Language and Culture at Università per Stranieri di Siena from April to June 2019. She graduated Magna cum Laude in 2018 and has a Bachelor of Arts degree in European Languages from the University of the Philippines, Diliman.
